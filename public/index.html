Damascus

Damascus patterns are known for their distinctive wavy, mottled, or fluid-like designs created by folding and forging multiple layers of metal. Traditionally associated with Damascus steel, this technique was historically used for high-quality sword blades, prized for their strength, sharpness, and resilience.

Metal Work

Metalwork involves various techniques like forging, casting, engraving, and soldering to create functional and decorative objects. It requires specialized tools such as hammers, anvils, torches, and molds, depending on the method used. Metals like iron, copper, silver, and gold are commonly shaped through heat, pressure, or chemical treatments to achieve desired forms and finishes.
